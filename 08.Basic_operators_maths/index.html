Basic operators, math

Back in the day, when I went to school, I learned about a lot of operators in math. They can also be used in JavaScript to perform operations on values (operands)

This chapter covers these operators and concentrates on the JavaScript specific aspects that we did not learn in school.

Maths

The following maths operators are supported in JavaScript:

  • Addition +
  • Subtraction -
  • Multiplication *
  • Division /
  • Remainder %
  • Exponentiation **

The first four are very straightforward, which is less the case for the Remainder (%) and Exponentiation (**)

Remainder (%)

Although there is a % sign in this operator, it has nothing to do with percents. It is used to find the remainder of a division between two integers.

The result of a % b is what remains after dividing a by b.

Exponentiation (**)

The exponentiation operator in 'a ** b' is used to raise a to the power of b

Example: 2 ** 4 = 2*2*2*2 = 16
